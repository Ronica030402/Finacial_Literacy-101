Introduction to how technology can help sustain small businesses

How is IoT Relevant for Small Businesses?

Firstly let's start by explaning what is IoT(Internet of Things)


The internet of things, or IoT, is a network of connected computing devices, mechanical and digital
machines, objects, animals, or people that can exchange data over a network without requiring hum
-an-to-human or human-to-computer interaction.

IoT devices easily remove process restrictions and solve conventional business challenges. As a result,
firms are able to optimize everything, experience more production, cut costs, and do a lot more.
Consider these 4 typical business needs that IoT provides excellent support for.

  1. Access Control

    2. Internet-enabled smart locks can be useful for manufacturing and retail businesses. These
    are the ideal instances of how IoT effortlessly resolves complex company issues.
    Businesses may regulate access to physical places without the usual limitations thanks
    to smart locks. Smart locks enable anything with just a few clicks, including changing
    passwords from a distance, tracking all accesses, and easily canceling access.

  2. Monitoring/Event Recording

    3. Smart cameras are an excellent example of how IoT devices can help small businesses.
    These Internet-connected video cameras may capture footage in high definition and
    upload it to the cloud. As a result, companies are spared the expense of purchasing
    closed-circuit cameras, which can cost between R1,000 and R4,000 each. Any company
    that needs to record extensive amounts of video cannot ignore the cost savings.

  3. Dynamic Inventory Management

    4. The usefulness of smart RFID tags for companies that deal in fast-moving consumer
    goods (FMCGs) is enormous. These little gadgets show how IoT can give businesses
    productivity advantages that have never been seen before.You can affix these
    reasonably priced RFID tags on goods. Then, portable devices can document
    activities like sales, returns, quarantine for quality assurance, etc. This
    significantly accelerates retail operations.

  4. Equipment Maintenance

    5. For many small firms, downtime is the scourge of their lives. Equipment failures
    typically take many hours to recover from, and they cost businesses on average
    R10,000 per hour.Imagine being able to do upkeep and repairs before any downtime
    occurs! That sounds magical, doesn't it? IoT sensors have that kind of power.
    IoT sensors can alert small businesses to an approaching breakdown so they can
    appoint a technician in advance to minimize downtime when they are configured
    appropriately. This is preferable to trying to control everything after a collapse
    has already happened.
